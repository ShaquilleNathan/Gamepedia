  • Game ID: Dota 2
  • Genre: Strategy
  • Multi-tags: Moba, Weapon, Strategy

Dota 2 is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) video game developed and published by Valve Corporation. The game is a sequel to Defense of the Ancients (DotA), a custom map for Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos and its expansion pack, The Frozen Throne. Dota 2 was released for Windows, macOS, and Linux in July 2013, following a beta period that began in 2011. The game is supported by microtransactions.


In Dota 2, players control a hero with unique abilities and battle against other players or computer-controlled opponents in team-based matches. Each match takes place on a symmetrical map, with each team occupying one side. The objective of the game is to destroy the opposing team's ancient, a powerful structure located in each team's base. Dota 2 features a large roster of heroes, each with their own unique abilities and playstyles. Heroes are divided into two main roles: carry and support. Carry heroes are typically responsible for dealing damage, while support heroes provide utility and protection for their team. Players can choose from a variety of heroes to create a team composition that suits their playstyle and strategy.

Overall, Dota 2 is a masterpiece of strategic depth and competitive gameplay. However, its high learning curve, demanding nature, and potential for toxicity should be considered before diving in. If you're willing to invest the time and effort, Dota 2 can offer an unparalleled level of gameplay satisfaction and a community that's as passionate as it is complex.
